# Pyrenees Hiking Itinerary: Hendaye Area, 14-20 July 2026

## Summary

- **Recommended route:** GR10 Hendaye → Col d'Ibardin → Sare → Ainhoa → Bidarray → Saint-Étienne-de-Baïgorry → Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port
- **Date range:** 14 July (depart) → 19 July (arrive Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port) · return 20 July
- **Total walking days:** 6
- **Total distance:** ~98 km
- **Start transport:** TER Bayonne–Hendaye (~30 min) or TGV Paris Montparnasse → Bayonne + TER to Hendaye
- **Return transport:** TER Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port → Bayonne (~1 h), then TGV/Intercités Bayonne → Paris
- **Main uncertainty:** Day 2 distance (Col d'Ibardin → Sare) may reach 20–22 km depending on variant — verify with current GR10 topoguide before departure. Ainhoa lodging capacity is very limited since Harazpy gîte permanently closed.

## Day 1 — Tuesday 14 July: Hendaye → Col d'Ibardin

- **Date:** 14 July 2026
- **From:** Hendaye (plage / station)
- **To:** Col d'Ibardin (Urrugne, 317 m)
- **Distance:** ~16 km
- **Elevation:** +700 m / −500 m
- **Estimated walking time:** 4 h 30
- **Route notes:** GR10 leaves from Hendaye beach, climbs through Biriatou and beech forest to reach the Col d'Ibardin, the French–Spanish border. The col is known for Ventas (Spanish duty-free shops) immediately on the Spanish side. A clear, well-marked path throughout.
- **Sites/views:** Hendaye coastline from height, Biriatou village (one of Basque Country's prettiest), La Rhune silhouette, Atlantic panorama.

- **Accommodation:**
  - **Name:** Camping Sunêlia Col d'Ibardin
  - **Type:** 4-star campsite (tent pitch) — no gîte d'étape at the col; this is the only confirmed walker-accessible overnight
  - **Price:** From €22/pitch/night (2026 rack rate, low season; July peak likely higher — contact to confirm)
  - **Availability:** Open 3 April – 27 September 2026 ✓ covers 14 July
  - **Booking/contact:** https://camping-ibardin-paysbasque.com · +33 5 59 54 31 56 (confirm by phone for a single tent pitch)
  - **Source:** tourisme64.com · camping-ibardin-paysbasque.com (checked June 2026)
  - **Confidence:** MEDIUM — campsite confirmed open and taking bookings; no gîte dorm available at the col. Consider this a tent night; bring a sleeping mat and bag or book in advance.

## Day 2 — Wednesday 15 July: Col d'Ibardin → Sare

- **Date:** 15 July 2026
- **From:** Col d'Ibardin (317 m)
- **To:** Sare (70 m)
- **Distance:** ~18–22 km (variant-dependent — see note)
- **Elevation:** +700 m / −950 m (includes La Rhune ridge traverse)
- **Estimated walking time:** 5 h 20 – 6 h
- **Route notes:** The GR10 passes briefly into Spain, loops back via Olhette, climbs to Col des Trois Fontaines with views of La Rhune, then descends to Sare. **Distance warning:** some sources quote this leg at 20–22 km; if uncomfortable, consider splitting at Olhette (gîte d'Olhette, ~8 km from col) and making Sare a shorter Day 3. Verify with current topoguide or GR10.org before departure.
- **Sites/views:** Ventas shops at the col, La Rhune cogwheel railway summit (if detour taken), Basque farmhouses above Sare.

- **Accommodation:**
  - **Name:** Gîte de la Petite Rhune
  - **Type:** Gîte d'étape (part of La Petite Rhune camping complex)
  - **Price:** ~€16–20/night dortoir (2015 last confirmed rate on refuges.info; current rate not published — contact required)
  - **Availability:** Not confirmed online for July 2026 — **book by phone or email before trip**
  - **Booking/contact:** contact@lapetiterhune.com · 06 15 29 36 38 · 05 59 54 23 97 · lapetiterhune.com/locations/gites/
  - **Address:** Quartier Lehenbiscay, Maison Esponda, 64310 Sare
  - **Source:** refuges.info/point/4837 · tourisme64.com (checked June 2026)

## Day 3 — Thursday 16 July: Sare → Ainhoa

- **Date:** 16 July 2026
- **From:** Sare (70 m)
- **To:** Ainhoa (120 m)
- **Distance:** ~13 km
- **Elevation:** +350 m / −300 m
- **Estimated walking time:** 3 h 10 – 3 h 30
- **Route notes:** Easy, shorter day through rolling Basque hills. Sare and Ainhoa are both classified among France's most beautiful villages ("Plus Beaux Villages de France"). Good day to rest legs before the longer Day 4.

## Day 4 — Friday 17 July: Ainhoa → Bidarray

- **Date:** 17 July 2026
- **From:** Ainhoa (120 m)
- **To:** Bidarray (105 m)
- **Distance:** ~19 km
- **Elevation:** +750 m / −760 m (includes Col des Trois Croix)
- **Estimated walking time:** 5 h 10
- **Route notes:** The longest ascent of this section — GR10 climbs steeply to Col des Trois Croix (~550 m), then descends a long ridge into the Nive valley at Bidarray. The descent can be hard on knees; poles recommended. No services on the ridge.
- **Sites/views:** Col des Trois Croix panorama, the Iparla ridge in the distance, Bidarray's arched bridge over the Nive.

- **Accommodation:**
  - **Name:** Arteka Menditarrena (Gîtes Menditarrena & Etxezaharria)
  - **Type:** Gîte d'étape — 79 places across two buildings; dorm beds and rooms
  - **Price:** €21/night individual dorm · €5 breakfast · meals available on request
  - **Availability:** Open year-round ✓ (confirmed 01/01/2026 – 31/12/2026). Individual walkers explicitly welcome.
  - **Booking/contact:** artekagr10@orange.fr · +33 5 59 37 71 34 · arteka-eh.com
  - **Address:** Bourg du village, 64780 Bidarray
  - **Source:** tourisme64.com (checked June 2026)
  - **Confidence:** HIGH — full details confirmed on official tourism platform; individual night rate published

## Day 5 — Saturday 18 July: Bidarray → Saint-Étienne-de-Baïgorry

- **Date:** 18 July 2026
- **From:** Bidarray (105 m)
- **To:** Saint-Étienne-de-Baïgorry (106 m)
- **Distance:** ~16 km
- **Elevation:** +800 m / −800 m
- **Estimated walking time:** 5 h 25
- **Route notes:** Crosses the Crêtes d'Irouléguy wine country above the Aldudes valley. Several route variants; the standard GR10 is well-marked. Saint-Étienne-de-Baïgorry is a pleasant Basque village with shops and a bridge.
- **Sites/views:** Irouléguy vineyard views, Aldudes valley, village church, the Nive des Aldudes river.

## Day 6 — Sunday 19 July: Saint-Étienne-de-Baïgorry → Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port

- **Date:** 19 July 2026
- **From:** Saint-Étienne-de-Baïgorry (106 m)
- **To:** Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port (183 m)
- **Distance:** ~18 km
- **Elevation:** +860 m / −860 m
- **Estimated walking time:** 6 h 15
- **Route notes:** The most demanding day elevation-wise, crossing Col Hargina (~900 m) before the final descent into Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port. The town is the traditional starting point of the Camino Francés to Santiago; expect crowds. Finish at the citadel for the view.
- **Sites/views:** Basque countryside panoramas, Col Hargina ridge, Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port's medieval walls and citadel.

- **Accommodation (night before return — 19 July):**
  - **Name:** Gîte Zazpiak Bat

## Transport

### Arrival (14 July)

- **Paris → Hendaye:** TGV or Intercités Paris Montparnasse → Bayonne (~4 h 45 – 5 h 30), then TER Bayonne → Hendaye (~30 min). Total: ~5.5 h. Book on sncf-connect.com.
- **Bayonne → Hendaye TER:** Frequent service; ~30 min, ~€5–8. Check ter.sncf.com/nouvelle-aquitaine for July timetable (not yet published as of June 2026 — recheck in early July).
- **Hendaye station** is ~1.5 km from the beach/GR10 start. Walk or taxi.

### Return (20 July)

- **Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port → Bayonne TER:** ~1 h; ~€8–10. Multiple trains daily. Timetable: sncf-connect.com/train/horaires/saint-jean-pied-de-port/bayonne
- **Bayonne → Paris:** TGV/Intercités as above. Book in advance for Bastille Day weekend (14 July) — trains fill up.
- **⚠ Note:** Exact July 2026 TER timetables were not accessible at time of research (June 2026). Verify schedules on ter.sncf.com closer to departure.

## Alternatives

### If Day 2 distance is too long (Col d'Ibardin → Sare)
Split at **Olhette** (~8 km from Col d'Ibardin, ~12 km from Sare):
- Gîte d'Olhette: reported by multiple hikers as a viable stopping point. Verify current status — no confirmed active listing found during this research. Search "Gîte d'Olhette GR10" before departing.
- This turns the trip into 7 days with an additional night at Olhette.

### If Kampo Ederrena is full (Ainhoa, Day 3)
Ainhoa has very limited hiker accommodation since Harazpy closed. Options:
1. Call Ohantzea (06 43 29 27 01) or Tartea (05 59 03 82 38)
2. Make Day 3 shorter by staying at Sare one more night and rescheduling
3. Check for any new listings on gites-refuges.com closer to departure

### If unable to finish in 6 days
Saint-Étienne-de-Baïgorry has a TER bus connection to Bayonne — this is a viable early exit point if weather or injury forces a stop.
